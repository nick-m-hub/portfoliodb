The **No-Brainer Portfolio** is one of William Bernstein's simplest and most widely cited model allocations. Bernstein, a neurologist turned financial theorist and author of books including [*The Four Pillars of Investing*](https://www.amazon.com/Four-Pillars-Investing-Building-Portfolio/dp/0071747052) and *The Intelligent Asset Allocator*, first described the No-Brainer Portfolio to illustrate that a straightforward, low-cost, four-fund allocation can outperform most actively managed portfolios over the long run. The portfolio holds equal 25% positions in US large-cap stocks, US small-cap stocks, international stocks, and short-term bonds.\n\n## Investment Philosophy\nBernstein's philosophy holds that the single most important determinant of investment success is the stock-bond split, and that within equities, simple diversification across market segments — including small-cap stocks, which carry a well-documented return premium — is more important than manager selection. The No-Brainer's equal weighting across four funds reflects the view that equal weighting is simple, easy to maintain, and avoids the need to make precise judgements about optimal weights. The short-term bond allocation keeps interest rate risk low while providing a buffer against equity volatility.\n\n## Who It's For\nThis portfolio is suited to investors who want a simple, evidence-grounded approach without complexity or ongoing management. The 75% equity allocation makes it appropriate for investors with a medium-to-long time horizon and moderate-to-high risk tolerance. It is particularly appropriate for investors who want some small-cap exposure beyond a plain total market fund without committing to a full factor-tilted strategy.\n\n## Pros\n- Extremely simple: just four equally weighted funds\n- Includes small-cap equity exposure, which has historically delivered a return premium over large-cap stocks\n- Short-term bonds keep interest rate risk minimal while still providing portfolio ballast\n\n## Cons\n- Equal weighting across the four funds is arbitrary and may not suit all risk profiles\n- 75% equity allocation is aggressive and will produce large drawdowns in severe bear markets\n- No international bond exposure or alternative asset classes beyond the four core funds
