**JL Collins Wealth Preservation Portfolio** was designed by J.L. Collins, author of [The Simple Path to Wealth](https://jlcollinsnh.com) and one of the most widely read voices in the FIRE (financial independence/early retirement) community. Collins developed this four-fund allocation for investors who have already built substantial wealth and now want to protect it -- while still growing it modestly without taking on unnecessary risk.\n\n## Investment Philosophy\nThe portfolio is built on Collins's core belief that simplicity is an edge. By holding just four broadly diversified, low-cost index funds, it avoids the complexity and costs that drag down more elaborate strategies. The equity sleeve provides long-term growth, real estate adds inflation protection and income, bonds smooth out volatility during downturns, and a small cash position covers near-term spending needs without forcing the sale of stocks at inopportune times. The result is a portfolio designed to let you sleep at night without sacrificing the growth needed to stay ahead of inflation.\n\n## Who It's For\nThis portfolio is best suited for investors within 5--10 years of retirement or already in retirement who have accumulated significant assets and want to preserve them. It works well for someone who found the all-stock approach too volatile but doesn't want to give up growth entirely. Compared to the [United States 60/40 Portfolio](/portfolios/united-states-60-40-portfolio), it trades bond weight for a large real estate sleeve that Collins believes hedges both inflation and deflation better over long periods.\n\n## Pros\n- Very simple to manage -- just four holdings, rebalanced annually\n- Real estate allocation historically hedges inflation better than bonds alone\n- Small cash buffer reduces pressure to sell equities during market downturns\n- Low cost when implemented with broad index ETFs\n\n## Cons\n- Real estate sector can suffer steep drawdowns (as in 2007--2009) and adds volatility\n- No international equity exposure -- fully concentrated in US markets\n- 5% cash creates a long-term drag on returns in sustained bull markets\n- Heavy equity + REIT allocation may still be too volatile for very conservative retirees
