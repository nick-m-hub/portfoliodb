The **Paul Merriman 4-Fund United States Portfolio** is a purely US equity portfolio designed by investing educator and former financial advisor Paul Merriman, founder of the [Paul Merriman Foundation](https://paulmerriman.com). Merriman built this portfolio as a straightforward entry point into factor-based investing -- four equal slices across the US equity style box, combining the S&P 500 with targeted exposure to the size and value premiums.\n\n## Investment Philosophy\nThe portfolio divides US equity exposure equally across large-cap blend, large-cap value, small-cap blend, and small-cap value. This equal weighting deliberately overweights small-cap and value stocks relative to a market-cap-weighted index, embedding the factor tilts that Fama-French research links to higher expected long-run returns. The design is a simplified cousin of Merriman's larger multi-asset strategies -- see [Ultimate Buy and Hold Portfolio 7](/portfolios/ultimate-buy-and-hold-portfolio-7-paul-merriman) for the global version with seven asset classes. Here the focus stays entirely on US equities, making the portfolio straightforward to implement and rebalance once a year.\n\n## Who It's For\nThis portfolio suits long-horizon investors who want meaningful exposure to the US size and value factors without international diversification or any fixed income. It is appropriate for investors comfortable holding 100% US equities and who believe the documented factor premiums will persist over their investment horizon. It is not suited to investors who need income, a volatility buffer, or exposure beyond the US market.\n\n## Pros\n- Captures four distinct US equity risk premiums (market, large value, small blend, small value) in a simple equal-weight structure\n- Annual rebalancing across four low-cost index funds is easy to maintain\n- Merriman's methodology is extensively documented -- a robust academic foundation underpins every allocation decision\n\n## Cons\n- 100% US equity allocation with no bonds or international diversification increases concentration risk\n- Small-cap and value tilts can significantly underperform the S&P 500 for extended periods, requiring conviction to stay the course\n- Factor premiums are not guaranteed -- there have been multi-decade windows where small-cap value has lagged
