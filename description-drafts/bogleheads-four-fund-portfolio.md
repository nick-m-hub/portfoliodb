The **Bogleheads Four-Fund Portfolio** extends the [Bogleheads Three-Fund Portfolio](/portfolios/bogleheads-three-fund-portfolio) by splitting its bond allocation between a broad bond market fund and an inflation-protected bond fund. Popularised by the Bogleheads community -- a group of index investing advocates inspired by Vanguard founder John Bogle -- this variant appears on the [Bogleheads wiki](https://www.bogleheads.org/wiki/Lazy_portfolios) as one of several common extensions to the original three-fund template.\n\n## Investment Philosophy\nThe core philosophy is unchanged from the three-fund portfolio: own the total stock and bond markets at minimal cost and let broad diversification do the work. The fourth fund addresses a specific gap that a standard total bond market fund doesn't fully cover -- a sustained rise in inflation that erodes the real value of fixed-rate bond payments. By carving out a portion of the bond sleeve into inflation-protected securities, the portfolio aims to preserve the purchasing power of its fixed-income holdings without changing the overall stock-to-bond split.\n\n## Who It's For\nThis portfolio suits the same investor as the three-fund version -- someone who values simplicity, low costs, and a hands-off approach -- but who wants explicit protection against inflation eating into their bond returns. It's a particularly natural fit for investors approaching or in retirement, when bond income makes up a larger share of total returns and inflation has more time to compound against a fixed payment stream.\n\n## Pros\n- Adds a measure of inflation protection to the bond sleeve without changing the overall stock/bond split\n- Retains nearly all the simplicity of the three-fund portfolio -- just one additional fund to track\n- Inflation-protected bonds have historically held up better than nominal bonds during unexpected inflation spikes\n\n## Cons\n- Inflation protection applies only to the portion of the bond allocation set aside for it, so the effect on the whole portfolio is modest\n- Inflation-protected bonds can lag nominal bonds during periods of falling or low inflation\n- Adds a small amount of complexity and one more fund to monitor and rebalance\n\n## Technical Notes\nInvestors can adjust how much of the bond allocation goes toward inflation protection based on their own inflation expectations and time horizon -- there's no fixed rule for the split.
