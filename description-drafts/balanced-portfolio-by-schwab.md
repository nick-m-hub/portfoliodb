The **Balanced Portfolio by Schwab** is one of several model portfolios published by Charles Schwab as part of its [Schwab Intelligent Portfolios](https://www.schwab.com/intelligent-portfolios) and general investment guidance offerings. It targets a roughly equal split between equities and fixed income, providing a middle-ground approach for investors who want growth exposure without committing fully to stocks. The portfolio uses broad index funds across domestic and international asset classes.\n\n## Investment Philosophy\nSchwab's balanced model is grounded in modern portfolio theory: diversification across asset classes reduces volatility without proportionally sacrificing return. The roughly even equity-to-bond weighting is designed to smooth out the ride during equity market downturns while still participating meaningfully in long-term growth. It reflects the conventional wisdom that a mix of stocks and bonds is appropriate for investors with intermediate time horizons who cannot stomach the full volatility of an all-equity portfolio.\n\n## Who It's For\nThis portfolio suits investors with a medium risk tolerance who have a time horizon of roughly five to fifteen years and want a simple, low-cost solution without active management. It is appropriate for someone saving toward a medium-term goal, such as retirement in a decade or a significant future expense.\n\n## Pros\n- Simple, broadly diversified structure that is easy to implement and maintain\n- Low-cost index fund approach keeps fees minimal\n- Balanced equity-bond split provides meaningful downside cushion relative to an all-equity portfolio\n\n## Cons\n- The equal stock-bond split may be too conservative for younger investors with long time horizons\n- Bond allocation introduces interest rate risk that can hurt nominal returns in rising-rate environments\n- Limited factor exposure means it captures only market beta, leaving potential risk-adjusted returns on the table
