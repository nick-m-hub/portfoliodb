The **GTAA AGG 6** portfolio is the moderate-concentration variant in Meb Faber's aggressive Global Tactical Asset Allocation series, introduced in the 2013 update to his foundational paper [*A Quantitative Approach to Tactical Asset Allocation*](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=962461). It uses the same 13-asset universe and composite momentum scoring as [GTAA AGG 3](/portfolios/global-tactical-asset-allocation-agg-3-meb-faber) but selects the top six ranked assets rather than three, providing a middle ground between the high concentration of AGG 3 and the full-universe diversification of [GTAA 13](/portfolios/global-tactical-asset-allocation-13-gtaa-13-meb-faber).\n\n## Investment Philosophy\nEach month, the strategy computes a composite momentum score for all 13 assets by averaging their trailing 1-, 3-, 6-, and 12-month returns. The top six receive equal allocations of one-sixth each. The same absolute momentum filter applies as in the rest of the GTAA series: any selected asset trading below its 10-month moving average is replaced with cash. By holding six of 13 assets, the portfolio captures a meaningful concentration advantage over a full equal-weight model while maintaining more diversification than the three-position AGG 3 approach.\n\n## Who It's For\nThis portfolio suits investors who want significant momentum exposure without the extreme single-asset concentration risk of GTAA AGG 3. It is appropriate for investors with a long time horizon and meaningful risk tolerance who want a systematic, evidence-based approach. Compared to AGG 3, it is better suited for investors who want to remain diversified across the assets they hold rather than betting entirely on the top three.\n\n## Pros\n- Six-asset allocation captures strong momentum trends while distributing risk across more positions than AGG 3\n- Absolute momentum filter avoids holding assets in clear downtrends\n- Based on Meb Faber's published research with extensive historical backtesting\n\n## Cons\n- Still relatively concentrated -- holding fewer than half of the 13-asset universe means significant exposure to whichever sectors dominate the momentum rankings\n- Higher portfolio turnover than diversified buy-and-hold approaches\n- Momentum strategies can lag in trendless, choppy, or rapidly reversing markets\n\n## Technical Notes\nThe 13-asset universe is identical to GTAA AGG 3: US and international equity subcategories, US and foreign government bonds, corporate bonds, commodities, gold, and US REITs. Composite momentum scores average the 1-, 3-, 6-, and 12-month returns. Assets below their 10-month simple moving average are replaced with cash. Rebalancing occurs monthly on the last trading day.
