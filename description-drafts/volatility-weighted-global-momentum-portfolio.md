**The Volatility-Weighted Global Momentum Portfolio** is a tactical strategy that rotates each month among eight global asset classes, holding only the three with the strongest recent momentum and sizing each position by its own volatility. It belongs to the [adaptive asset allocation](/portfolios/adaptive-asset-allocation) family, pairing trend-based selection with risk-based weighting in a single rules-driven system.\n\n## Investment Philosophy\nThe strategy rests on two well-documented ideas. First, assets that have performed well over the recent past tend to keep outperforming, so ranking a broad opportunity set by momentum concentrates capital where trends are strongest -- the same selection logic behind strategies like [GEM Dual Momentum](/portfolios/gem-dual-momentum). Second, weighting the chosen holdings by the inverse of their volatility gives calmer assets a larger share and more turbulent assets a smaller one, so no single position dominates the portfolio's risk. Momentum is measured across several lookback windows at once, weighted toward the intermediate ones, so the ranking reflects both fresh shifts and more durable trends. The result is a portfolio that leans into whichever corner of the global market is working -- equities, treasuries, gold, or energy -- while stepping back from assets that are turning erratic.\n\n## Who It's For\nSuited to investors who are comfortable holding a concentrated, frequently changing set of positions and who value drawdown control over tracking a fixed benchmark. Because the mix can swing from fully invested in equities to heavily defensive within a single month, it asks for tolerance for looking very different from a traditional stock-and-bond portfolio. A minimum horizon of five years is recommended so the approach has room to work across a full market cycle.\n\n## Pros\n- Concentrates in the strongest-trending assets while sidestepping sustained downtrends\n- Volatility-based weighting balances risk contributions rather than dollar amounts\n- Draws from a broad global menu, so it is rarely forced to hold a falling market\n\n## Cons\n- Concentrated three-asset holdings can lag a diversified portfolio when leadership rotates quickly\n- Momentum signals whipsaw in choppy, trendless markets, generating turnover without benefit\n- Requires disciplined monthly rebalancing across several positions\n\n## Technical Notes\nEach month the eight candidate asset classes are scored by a blended momentum measure and the top three are selected. Those three are then weighted in inverse proportion to their recent volatility, so the least volatile of the chosen assets receives the largest allocation. The portfolio is fully rebalanced at each month-end.
